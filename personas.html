"Suffering Sandra"

The Dry Eye Patient

Demographics

Age45-65
GenderFemale (more common in women)
Income$60,000-100,000
LocationWithin 45-minute drive
OccupationProfessional, remote worker, retiree

Eye Care Needs

  • Advanced dry eye diagnosis and treatment
  • IPL, thermal pulsation treatments
  • Meibomian gland evaluation
  • Ongoing dry eye management

Psychographics

  • Dealing with symptoms for years
  • Frustrated that drops don't work
  • Willing to travel for specialized care
  • Actively researching online

Pain Points

"I've tried every eye drop and nothing works long-term."
"My regular eye doctor just tells me to use drops more often."
"My eyes burn and itch every day, especially in winter."

Messaging That Resonates

"Finally. Real relief for dry eyes."

"Beyond drops: Advanced dry eye treatment"

"Our Dry Eye Spa gets to the root cause."

Primary - Highest Volume

"Concerned Parent Paula"

The Family Eye Care Decision-Maker

Demographics

Age35-50
GenderFemale (healthcare decision-maker)
Income$65,000-110,000
LocationHolden, surrounding school districts
FamilyChildren ages 5-18

Eye Care Needs

  • Pediatric eye exams
  • Myopia management (screen time concerns)
  • Family scheduling convenience
  • Quality children's frames

Psychographics

  • Makes healthcare decisions for entire family
  • Concerned about children's vision and screen time
  • Prefers one practice for whole family
  • Active in local community and school groups

Pain Points

"I'm worried about all the screen time and my kids' eyes."
"I want a doctor who will really check my children's vision."
"I've heard myopia is getting worse in kids - is there anything we can do?"
Secondary

"Quality-Focused Quincy"

The Insurance-Frustrated Patient

Demographics

Age55-68
GenderMale or Female
Income$70,000-120,000
LocationHolden, Brewer, surrounding towns
OccupationProfessional, retired, self-employed

Eye Care Needs

  • Comprehensive medical eye care
  • Cataract monitoring
  • Diabetic eye care
  • Glaucoma/macular degeneration screening

Psychographics

  • Had bad experience with insurance-based care
  • Values thoroughness over speed
  • May have been misdiagnosed at chain practice
  • Word-of-mouth carries weight

Pain Points

"My last eye doctor spent 5 minutes with me and missed something."
"I'm tired of being told what's covered instead of what's best for me."
"Insurance practices are just trying to get you in and out."
Secondary

"Busy Professional Brian"

The Time-Conscious Executive

Demographics

Age45-55
GenderMale
Income$85,000-150,000+
LocationBangor metro area
OccupationBusiness owner, manager, professional

Eye Care Needs

  • Progressive lenses (presbyopia)
  • Annual comprehensive exams
  • Premium lens options
  • Early dry eye symptoms (screen time)

Pain Points

"I don't have time to sit in a waiting room for an hour."
"I'm paying for insurance but still wait forever and feel like a number."
"I'd rather pay more and actually talk to my doctor."

Messaging That Resonates

  • "Your time is valuable. So is your vision."
  • "No insurance hassle. Just expert care."
  • "The thorough exam you deserve, on your schedule."
Tertiary

"Contact Lens Connie"

The Active Lifestyle Patient

Demographics

Age28-45
GenderFemale or Male
Income$55,000-90,000
LocationBangor metro area
LifestyleActive (hiking, sports, outdoors)

Eye Care Needs

  • Contact lens fitting and annual exams
  • Specialty lenses (daily, toric, multifocal)
  • Backup glasses with quality frames
  • Dry eye from contact wear

Pain Points

"My contacts from online never fit as well as doctor-fit ones."
"I get dry eyes from my contacts, especially at end of day."
"I want contacts that work for my active lifestyle."

Messaging That Resonates

  • "Contact lenses that keep up with your life."
  • "Expert fitting for all-day comfort."
  • "The difference a proper fitting makes."
